Volunteering means giving your time and abilities to help others without the want of anything. Put simply, volunteering is a service to your community. While you won't get paid for your community service, volunteering comes with a lot of other benefits. Donating your time, energy, and resources can be just as rewarding for you as for those you are helping. Here are some ways volunteering can affect you.

Meet new people and build a community
Everyone loves new friends! Volunteering means helping people, and that means you'll be creating meaningful relationships with others and increasing your social activities. You're sure to meet people and make new friends that share common interests, values, and opinions. You will build a community in which you are all working together towards common future goals.

Get knowledge and understanding of other ways of life
Volunteering might take you to a new part of your community you have never been to before. Volunteer programmes can give you the opportunity to bring people into your social network. You can get to meet and learn from those who come from different walks of life. This experience can expand your understanding of others who are different from you. Learning about different cultures and ways of life can help you know better about the world.

Gain a sense of purpose and become happier
What an amazing reason to do something! Who doesn't want to become happier? Volunteering has been shown to give you a sense of accomplishment and to increase your feelings of happiness. When you help somebody in need, it is like realizing one of your dreams and you are sure to feel happier in your heart.
