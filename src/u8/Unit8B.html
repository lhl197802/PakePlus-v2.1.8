Going green is a great way to reduce pollution and help the earth. Being green is all about reducing how many resources you use, reusing things when you can, and recycling things that can't be reused. If you're ready to go green, start by changing your personal habits.

First, you can carry a reusable water bottle. Bottled water is convenient, but it is also bad for the environment. Not only does it create rubbish, but it also uses more resources because it is sent to the store. To be more eco-friendly, make it a ____ to carry a reusable water bottle with you everywhere so that you can drink water at any time.

Second, you can use reusable bags while you're shopping. Shopping bags make it easy to carry your things home, but they also pollute the earth. Don't use the store's shopping bags; bring your own instead. Use a backpack that you already own, or buy some reusable bags from your local grocery store. You can also use your reusable bags at most stores, including clothing stores or bookstores.

Third, you can reduce the time of using electronics. While electronics are really fun and likely help you feel connected to your friends, they also use a lot of energy. Take breaks from your electronics so you can use them less. During your breaks, do something fun like going for a walk, playing a game with your friends, or finding a hobby.

Fourth, you can buy second-hand products or borrow things. Buying new things may produce more waste and pollution. So try not to buy things you don't need. When you do need something, shop in second-hand shops. If you can, borrow things that you don't use often, like tools, from a friend or family members.
