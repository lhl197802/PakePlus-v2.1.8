Australia is a large country. I didn't really know about its size until my 5-week-long trip down the country's east coast. I have never been there before. Now let me tell you some important and unforgettable events.

Snorkelling to the Great Barrier Reef
Taking a snorkelling day trip out onto the Great Barrier Reef is a must for any visitor to the state of Queensland in Australia's north-east. We snorkelled at three different sites throughout the day. We saw different kinds of fishes and beautiful coral. All of them are just in front of me, not a photo!

A Day Trip to Fraser Island
Fraser Island is the world's largest sand island. The best way of exploring this island is by 4WD, so we joined a small group for a tour. It was a busy but exciting day, with plenty of chances for swimming. We also experienced several hours of exciting driving over the sandy roads. At times, it was ____ to believe that we were on an island made of sand. Forests have grown well and the scenery is certainly a lot more beautiful than what I first expected!

Walking on the Blue Mountains
We made an overnight visit to the Blue Mountains. Forests of eucalyptus, with their special blue haze, were interspersed with wonderful waterfalls and rocks, and with the coral reef earlier in the trip, there were times when I couldn't quite believe that what I was seeing was real! Walking on the Blue Mountains, we were just like living in a different world.
